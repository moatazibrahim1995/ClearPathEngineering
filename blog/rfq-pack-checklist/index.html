Supplier-Ready RFQ Pack Checklist (Templates + Examples)

What to include so vendors quote quickly—without rework or “please clarify” emails. Use this to get apples-to-apples quotes.

The RFQ essentials

  • Scope & BOM: part list, revisions, quantities (prototype, pilot, production).
  • Drawings: clear views, units, datums, callouts, surface finishes.
  • Tolerances: general note + fit-critical features; avoid over-tightening.
  • Materials: grade/spec, color, hardness, certifications (UL/FCC/CE where relevant).
  • Processes: e.g., IM/Sheetmetal/CNC; draft, radii, parting line expectations.
  • Inspection: key dimensions, CMM/report needs, sampling plan.
  • Quantity & lead time: order size, target dates, split-ship allowed?
  • Packaging & shipping: packaging type, ship-to, Incoterms.
  • NDA: attach or note it’s required before files.
  • Response format: unit price, tooling/NRE, lead time, payment terms, notes.

Upstream check: run a quick DFM & tolerance review first. Fixing draft/undercuts now saves weeks later.

Copy-paste vendor email

Subject: RFQ – [Part/Assembly name] – [Quantity] – [Target date]

Body:

  • Attached: BOM + drawings (PDF), STEP files, RFQ checklist.
  • Please quote: unit price by quantity tier, tooling/NRE (if any), lead time, terms.
  • Notes: key tolerances, finish, inspection level, packaging.
  • Questions welcome—prefer one consolidated list.
  • Reply by: [date]. Thanks!

Attachments: BOM.csv, Drawings.pdf, STEP.zip, RFQ-Checklist.pdf (or link).

How to compare quotes (fast)

  • Line up columns: Unit price, Tooling/NRE, Lead time, MOQ, Terms, Notes.
  • Flag risks: long lead items, secondary ops, tolerance pushback.
  • Decide: pick one primary + one backup; document assumptions.
